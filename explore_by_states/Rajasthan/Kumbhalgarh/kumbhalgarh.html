Kumbhalgarh

HISTORY

Kumbhalgarh is a historically significant fortress located in the Rajsamand district of Rajasthan, India. Its history dates back to the 15th century when it was constructed by Rana Kumbha, a ruler of the Mewar region of Rajasthan. The fortress was built during the 15th century to serve as a defensive stronghold against invasions from neighboring states such as the Mughals and the Marathas.

Rana Kumbha, who ruled the Mewar region from 1433 to 1468, initiated the construction of Kumbhalgarh in the 15th century. The fortress was strategically located atop the Aravalli Range, providing a commanding view of the surrounding area. It was built over a period of several years, with extensive walls and bastions to fortify the structure

Architecture

Kumbhalgarh Fort is renowned for its massive walls, which are considered to be the second-longest continuous wall after the Great Wall of China. The walls extend over 36 kilometers and are 15 feet thick. The fortifications are punctuated by numerous bastions and watchtowers.

Apart from its architectural and historical significance, Kumbhalgarh Fort holds cultural importance as well. It was the birthplace of Maharana Pratap, one of the greatest warriors of Mewar, who fought against the Mughals.

TIMINGS

Kumbhalgarh Fort welcomes visitors from early morning until evening, typically opening around 9:00 AM and closing around 6:00 PM every day of the week. However, specific timings may vary, particularly for special events like the light and sound shows that illuminate the fort's history during the night. It's advisable to confirm operational hours and any closures beforehand to ensure a smooth visit.

NEARBY ATTRACTIONS

Without seeing the tranquil and stunning Neelkanth Mahadev Temple, a three-story Jain temple honouring goddess Vedi, your trip to Kumbhalgarh would not be complete. Additionally, you must visit the distinctive Parshuram temple, where Rishi Parshuram meditated. With the valiant Haldighati site, the legendary Chittorgarh, and the tiny town of Jawai that is home to the majestic felines, the locations to explore close to the places in Kumbhalgarh are as gratifying as they can possibly be. How to Get There

By Road

Kumbhalgarh is well-connected by road to major cities like Udaipur (about 85 km), Jodhpur (about 160 km), and Ahmedabad (about 375 km). State-run buses, as well as private taxis, are available from these cities to Kumbhalgarh.

By Train

The nearest railway station to Kumbhalgarh is Falna Railway Station, located at a distance of about 85 km. It is well-connected to major cities like Ahmedabad, Jaipur, and Delhi. From Falna, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Kumbhalgarh.

By Air

The nearest airport to Kumbhalgarh is Maharana Pratap Airport in Udaipur, located at a distance of about 110 km. This airport is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Jaipur. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Kumbhalgarh.
